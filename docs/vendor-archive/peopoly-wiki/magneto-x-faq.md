# [¶](#faq-for-magneto-x-3d-printer) FAQ for Magneto X 3D Printer

-
 What is the Magneto X?

The Magneto X is a desktop FFF (Fused Filament Fabrication) FDM (Fused Deposition Modeling) 3D printer designed for high precision and speed, thanks to its innovative magnetic levitation motion system.

-
 How much does the Magneto X cost?

The retail price of the Magneto X is USD $1999.

-
 When will the Magneto X be available for shipping?

We have began shipping to pre-orders. For estimated delivery date for your order, please contact [support@peopoly.net](mailto:support@peopoly.net)

-
 What is the warranty period for the Magneto X?

The Magneto X comes with a 1-year warranty on parts, applicable worldwide where applies.

-
 What is unique about the Magneto X's motion system?

The Magneto X features the MagXY system, a magnetic levitation linear motor for the X and Y axes, ensuring precise movement and high speeds with low surface artifacts.

-
 What are the maximum print and travel speeds of the Magneto X?

The maximum print speed is 1200mm/s for 0.1mm layer height, and the maximum travel speed is 1500mm/s. Actually print speed highly dependent on types of filament, meltzone size, and profile settings.

-
 What safety features does the Magneto X have?

It has an auto disengagement feature for the motor to prevent damage if the extruder runs into an object.

-
 What is the print volume of the Magneto X?

The print volume is 400 x 300 x 300 mm (X/Y/Z).

-
 Does the Magneto X have a heated build platform?

Yes, it has a 1000W AC heated aluminum build platform with a PEI magnetic flexible build surface.

-
 Can the Magneto X print with any type of filament?

It supports a wide range of materials including PLA, PETG, ABS, ASA, PA6, PA12, PA-CF, PC-GF, and more. We recommend official enclosure for engineering grade plastics.

-
 What is the maximum temperature the Magneto X's hot end can reach?

The hot end can reach up to 300°C.

-
 Does the Magneto X come with a filament runout sensor?

Yes, it includes a filament runout sensor with filament movement control.

-
 What type of leveling does the Magneto X use?

It uses 48 bed mesh points + auto-leveling via a load cell sensor in the extruder.

-
 What type of nozzle does the Magneto X use?

It uses a nozzle compatible with RepRap M6 / E3D V6 standards, made of hardened steel

-
 What is the power requirement for the Magneto X?

It requires a supply input of either 110V or 220V and has a max power output of 1950W.

-
 What kind of control system does the Magneto X use?

It uses an Orange Pi Zero 2 H616 system board and a BTT Octopus Pro 1.1 H712 control board, with Klipper firmware.

-
 Does the Magneto X have WiFi connectivity?

Yes, it has WiFi 802.11 N/AC and also supports Ethernet connectivity.

-
 What slicer software is compatible with the Magneto X?

It is compatible with Orca Slicer, PrusaSlicer, Bambu Slicer and Cura. We recommend OrcaSlicer.

-
 Does the Magneto X have any special features for monitoring prints?

Yes, it includes a 1080P 30FPS camera and is accessible via Klipper's Mainsail. You can access Mainsail via browser or OrcaSlicer if the computer and Magneto X is on the same network.

-
 Are there any optional accessories for the Magneto X?

Optional accessories includes enclosure and side panel fans.

-
 What should I do if my Magneto X is not turning on?

First, ensure that the printer is of the correct voltage. You can check system voltage by looking at the stick about the power switch. [pic] Also make sure the cable properly plugged into a working power outlet. Check the power cable for any damage. If the issue persists, contact our support team for further assistance.

-
 How can I unclog the nozzle of my Magneto X?

To unclog the nozzle, heat it to the recommended temperature for the filament you're using, then gently push a cleaning filament or a thin wire through the nozzle. If clogging is frequent, consider checking for and replacing the nozzle if it's worn out. [How to clear a filament jam](/en/magneto/magneto-x/magneto-x-nozzle-clogging)

-
 What maintenance does the Magneto X require?

Regular maintenance includes checking for loose screws, ensuring the motion system is clean and lubricated, cleaning the build plate, and occasionally checking the electrical connections for signs of wear or damage.

-
 How do I update the firmware on my Magneto X?

Download the latest firmware version by following this link [wiki link](/en/magneto/magneto-x/update-tf-image)

-
 My Magneto X is making unusual noises during operation. What should I do?

Unusual noises can be due to loose components or improper configuration on the motion system. Check and tighten any loose parts and apply lubricant to the rails. If the problem continues, contact support for a detailed diagnosis. Please note that Magneto X generates a low humming noise when linear motor is powered on. The motor can be switched off when the printer is on but not printing.

-
 The print bed of my Magneto X is not heating up. What could be the problem?

Check the connection between the build platform and the printer. If the connection is secure, the issue might be with the heating element or its control circuit. Avoid attempting to repair electrical components yourself and reach out to our support team.

-
 How do I replace the filament on the Magneto X?

To replace the filament, heat the extruder to the filament's recommended temperature, then press the release lever on the extruder and pull the old filament out. Insert the new filament until it's fed through the extruder. Please Please refer thi [Setup Guide](/en/magneto/magneto-x/setup-guide) 's charter 8.1

-
 What should I do if the auto-leveling feature is not working properly?

Ensure the build plate is clean and free of debris. Rerun the Quad Gantry Level and Bed Mesh and see if the works. Please contact support if problem persists

-
 How do I solve adhesion problems on the Magneto X's build plate?

First, make sure the build plate is clean and properly leveled. Remove any offset you may have usedr, rerun the quad gantry level and then bed mesh again and test to see if adhesion is better.
